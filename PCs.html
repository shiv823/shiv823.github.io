Semester Project ‚≠ê

Throughout the semester, my partner Nick and I worked on a social good computing project together with the intention of building more trust between donors and tree charities. We wanted to increase the number of donations tree charities receive and figured the best way to approach this was to allow users to see their monetary impact. The project had multiple components (9 total to be precise), each broken down this semester weekly.

Components 0-1 ‚ùó

Both component 0 and 1 can be described as a pre-project planning task. At this point, everyone in the class was thinking of personal ideas that would promote social good across a variety of topics. The ideas were then shared with classmates and teams formed based on interests and skills. Nick is the mastermind behind the TreeTracker project. I enjoyed listening to his presentation and we decided to work together! Here is the original padlet board of ideas where you can find Nick's project pitch: https://padlet.com/tug81053/eowih0g78662cs4b. We planned to create an application that promotes trust in charities thus boosting tree planting as a result. In terms of Rediet Abebe‚Äôs roles of computing for social justice, this project would best be understood as a synecdoche. Planting more trees and reducing tree cutting has been a social good topic for a long time, however utilizing technology with a focus on charity trust is a newer and more innovative approach.

Component 3: Group Proposal üë•

In project component 3, we expanded upon component 2. To summarize this part, we concluded has two important implementations: 1) figuring out how to web scrape data, and 2) creating a UI that is simple for the user to understand. Some milestones we decided on include: figuring out common tree planting charities, figuring out what libraries are needed for web scraping and creating a user interface, figuring out what user input will be needed, and then actually creating the application itself. We chose to forgo assigning each other roles and wanted to play on our strengths. Component 5: Intermediate Prototype Presentation üó£

In this component, teams presented their intermediate project prototype. I was not in class for the presentation unfortunately, but Nick presented on our team's behalf and discussed component 4 in front of the class. Nick wrote for his slide: Our project was going to be a tree planting tracker where whenever you donate to a nonprofit tree planting company, such as the Arbor Day Foundation it tells you where and what kind of trees were planted with your money. The goal was to create a platform where the user can enter any website they want, and the system will determine whether or not it‚Äôs a tree planting organization. The system will then tell the user what kind of trees were planted with their donation, and where they were planted.

Component 6: Our Updated Prototype + Project Presentation ‚öôÔ∏è

Component 6 is where we shifted gears and our idea was 100% solidified. It became clear that finding actual tree location data from charities was going to become a challenge. Instead of finding where individual trees got planted, we decided to show a general map of the impact every donation has. This was done using Global Forest Watch‚Äôs interactive forest map, which is a free online tool that shows tree losses and gains. Additionally, we switched over to Swift as our language of choice instead of Python, as almost 80% of the world uses a smartphone in 2022. Mobile donations have also risen by 205% in the last year alone. It was here that I decided it made sense to focus on charity trust, as tree planting increase would be a byproduct. I did research and learned that only 19% of American adults trust charities. I wanted to raise this number by showing the app's users that there are actually trees being planted, hence producing more donations and users.

For our final project implementation, we used Swift and Xcode to create an iOS application with a clean and user-friendly interface. We broke this part into 3 different steps: 1) Design the app‚Äôs UX using tools such as Swift, Adobe XD, and InVision. 2) Using Xcode, add in screens and use Swift for user data. and 3) Test and see if our app works

We contemplated adding a leaderboard system, as we learned while doing research that Gen Z (individuals born between 1997 and 2007) donates the most to charities. 87% of Gen Z plays video games too, so we thought making the app competitive would be fun for them.

I first approached the app by designing wireframes using pencil and paper. It's not very beautiful to look at, but it wasn't meant to be. I wanted to understand the general flow of the app before I began designing it.

Next, I designed the actual mockup in a free tool called Adobe XD. I've used Adobe XD while taking courses in the Tyler School of Art, so I already had some familiarity with it. I added visuals to the wireframe and confirmed the design. Shown below is one of my mockups:

Finally, after I knew visually what I wanted to achieve, I opened up the Xcode IDE and made my attempt at Swift by following various online tutorials. I used Xcode a few years ago, however it had been so long that I forgot almost everything. Luckily, it came back to me relatively fast! Down below are various pictures that show my work. In component 7, there is a video demonstration of my application.

As Swift was relatively new for me, I relied heavily on online resources to get my app up and running. It's not a perfect app, but with the time period, I think I was able to learn a lot, such as: Xcode storyboards, code to connect URLs to buttons, how to create a launch page, app transitions, connecting a data map, allowing users to enter information on the app, and navigating through the IDE.

Some things I was not able to implement: mail authentication, cross-platform ability, retaining user information on an account to implement a leaderboard system (not enough Swift experience and time), maybe a chat system to talk with other users? If possible, it would be nice to contact charities for data to implement our original tree tracking idea as a feature in this app.
